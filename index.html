The Movement Imagery Ability Task Project

This website is designed to provide an index of open source tasks used to measure movement imagery ability (i.e. how well people can imagine performing movements). We have outlined our proposals for why this is important in our preprint.

The Hand Laterality Judgement Task (HLJT)
Type: Mental Rotation
Dimension(s): Manipulation
Repository: https://osf.io/8h7ec/
Citation: Moreno-Verdú et al., 2025

The Hand Laterality Judgement Task (HLJT) was first introduced by Cooper and Shepard (1975), who proposed that participants "determine whether a visually presented hand is left or right by moving a mental "phantom" of one of their own hands into the portrayed position and by then comparing its imagined appearance against the appearance of the externally presented hand." Many variants of the task have subsequently been examined in the context of studies of motor imagery.

The "biomechanical constraints effect", an effect where hands presented in more physically easy to achieve (medial) rotations are processed faster than those with more difficult (lateral) rotations is considered to be a hallmark of the use of motor imagery in the task.

This version of the task, as developed by Moreno-Verdu et al., (2025), presents hands at 8 angles (0 through 3XX degrees in 45 degree increments) in a 'palmar' or 'dorsal' view. This implementaiton has been verified to provide equivalent results when performed with different effectors (e.g. the feet, bimanually, or unimanually), making it feasible for use with participants with impairments in a specific effector.
